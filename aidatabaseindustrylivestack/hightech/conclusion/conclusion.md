# Conclusion Product Intelligence Outcomes

## Introduction

This closing lab consolidates the High Tech Product Intelligence LiveStack story. The presenter should finish by showing how each scene contributes to a single customer-facing outcome: Seer Tech can serve trusted product intelligence through dashboards, data products, spatial operations, machine learning, natural-language access, and audited AI agents from one Oracle-centered foundation.

Estimated Time: 10 minutes

### Objectives

In this lab, you will:
- Review the final scene sequence and the outcome signal each scene contributes.
- Connect the visible application workflows to Oracle AI Database 26ai capabilities.
- Prepare a concise stakeholder narrative for product, operations, data, and AI audiences.

## Task 1: Review the Scene Sequence

1. Return to **Seer Tech Control Tower**.
2. Walk the audience through the left navigation from top to bottom.
3. For each scene, state the operator question it answers:
   - What product signals are emerging?
   - Which developer communities or advocates are influencing demand?
   - Where is availability or capacity at risk?
   - Which orders or products need action?
   - What do ML models predict?
   - What can users ask in natural language?
   - What can agents recommend or audit?

Expected result:
- The demo lands as a connected operational story instead of a collection of disconnected feature pages.
- The presenter can explain how each page contributes evidence to the product-intelligence decision.

## Task 2: Summarize Oracle-Backed Evidence

1. Name the Oracle capabilities used across the scenes: relational SQL, native JSON, JSON relational duality, vectors, property graph, spatial, VPD, Oracle Machine Learning, ORDS, and agent audit trails.
2. Explain where the audience saw each capability in the app.
3. Tie those capabilities to business outcomes: faster signal detection, better product prioritization, safer governed access, more explainable fulfillment decisions, and traceable AI assistance.

Expected result:
- Stakeholders understand which Oracle capabilities map to which visible workflows.
- The discussion stays anchored in business outcomes rather than feature inventory alone.

## Task 3: Why this matters?

High-tech product teams need to respond to market signals, availability constraints, and customer demand quickly, but they also need governed data and explainable decisions. This LiveStack shows how Oracle AI Database 26ai can keep operational data, AI search, spatial context, ML scoring, natural-language access, and agent workflows close together.
