# Scene 1 Seer Lifesciences Control Tower

## Introduction

The Control Tower is the entry point for the Seer Lifesciences story. It frames the regulated operations workflow as a single path from governed data ingestion to AI-assisted action, and it gives the presenter quick routes into the dashboard, data foundation, and Ask Data experience.

Estimated Time: 10 minutes

![Seer Lifesciences Control Tower screenshot](images/control-tower.png)

### Objectives

In this lab, you will:
- Orient the audience to the regulated supply, quality, and AI story.
- Use the quick route buttons to move into the main demo paths.
- Confirm that the left navigation, active dataset, user switcher, and customer name controls are available for the rest of the walkthrough.

## Task 1: Open the control tower

1. Open the LiveStack home page at `http://localhost:8505`.
2. Review the workflow stages: Ingest, Transform, Serve Data, Serve AI, and Govern.
3. Point out the quick route buttons for Dashboard, Schema and Data, and Ask Your Data.

Expected result:
- The audience sees the LiveStack as a connected regulated operations platform rather than a group of disconnected pages.
- The presenter has visible entry points into operational dashboards, schema inspection, and natural-language querying.

## Task 2: Personalize the demo customer

1. Open the **Customer name** control in the sidebar.
2. Enter a customer name if the demo needs to be retold for a specific account.
3. Leave **Save across sessions** off for one-time presentation changes, or enable it when preparing a persistent demo environment.

Expected result:
- Visible Seer Lifesciences labels are replaced with the selected customer name where the app supports customer text replacement.
- The demo can be framed for a customer without changing source code or database content.

## Task 3: Why this matters?

This scene sets the narrative. The business signal is that Oracle AI Database 26ai can support dashboards, apps, APIs, analytics, and AI workflows from the same governed data foundation, which is essential for regulated life sciences operations where data movement increases risk.
