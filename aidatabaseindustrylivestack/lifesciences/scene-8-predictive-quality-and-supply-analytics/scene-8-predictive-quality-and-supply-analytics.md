# Scene 8 Predictive Quality and Supply Analytics

## Introduction

The predictive analytics scene presents multiple Oracle Machine Learning and vector-backed views: product risk, trial site segments, supply forecast, product behavior clusters, and cold-chain capacity intelligence.

Estimated Time: 10 minutes

![Predictive Quality and Supply Analytics screenshot](images/predictive-analytics.png)

### Objectives

In this lab, you will:
- Switch between the predictive analytics tabs.
- Refresh model-backed views when the backend stack is running.
- Explain how in-database analytics can support quality, supply, and capacity decisions.

## Task 1: Review prediction tabs

1. Select **Predictive Quality & Supply Analytics**.
2. Move through the tab buttons: Product Risk, Trial Site Segments, Supply Forecast, Product Behavior Clusters, and Cold-Chain Capacity.
3. Review the charts, tables, confidence indicators, and model notes for each tab.

Expected result:
- The presenter can show multiple analytical views without leaving the application.
- The audience sees quality and supply predictions as part of the operator workflow, not as a disconnected data science artifact.

## Task 2: Refresh model-backed results

1. Use the refresh or run controls in the active tab when the backend is available.
2. For clustering, change the cluster count where the UI allows it and run the analysis again.
3. Compare the updated cards, charts, or tables to the previous state.

Expected result:
- The app updates model-backed outputs such as risk predictions, segments, forecasts, clusters, or capacity signals.
- The scene shows how in-database analytics can be rerun and inspected from the same governed app surface.

## Task 3: Why this matters?

Prediction is most useful when it is close to the operational decision. This scene shows how teams can use ML, vector clustering, and capacity intelligence to prioritize action before quality or supply issues become escalations.
